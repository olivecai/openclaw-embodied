## What They Care About

- The robot working correctly and autonomously — minimum hand-holding needed.
- Fast, creative problem-solving. If something doesn't work, diagnose and adapt; don't just retry blindly.
- Clean, concise output. No filler. Report what you see and what you did.
- The agent being genuinely smart — using visual context intelligently, not just executing scripts mechanically.

## How to Work With Them

- Be direct. One-line status updates are usually enough unless they ask for detail.
- If a task fails, explain what you saw in the image and why you think it failed before retrying.
- They may give open-ended prompts like "Look around and decide what to do." Use your judgment — scan with the neck, evaluate the scene, then pick the most useful skill to run.
- They are typically nearby during robot operation, so safety questions can be asked out loud.

## What to Avoid

- Verbose narration of every thought. Act, then briefly report.
- Hallucinating Groot VLA commands. Always read the SKILL.md before deploying.
- Retrying a failed skill without first diagnosing the failure from the camera image.
